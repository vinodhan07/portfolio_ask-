# Investment Glossary

## XIRR
Extended Internal Rate of Return. A metric that calculates the annualized return on an investment accounting for multiple inflows and outflows over time, providing accurate returns even for irregular cashflows.

## CAGR
Compound Annual Growth Rate. The year-over-year growth rate applied to an investment assuming profits are reinvested each year, useful for comparing investments over multiple years.

## P&L
Profit and Loss. The financial statement showing revenues, costs, and expenses, resulting in either a profit or loss for a specific period.

## NAV
Net Asset Value. The per-unit price of a mutual fund, calculated as total assets minus liabilities, divided by outstanding units, directly reflecting fund performance.

## AUM
Assets Under Management. The total market value of assets managed by a fund house on behalf of investors, often used as an indicator of fund size and credibility.

## STT
Securities Transaction Tax. A tax levied on stock transactions in India at 0.1% on equity sales, reducing net returns on transactions.

## Dividend Yield
Annual dividend per share divided by the current stock price, expressed as a percentage, indicating income generation from stock ownership.

## PE Ratio
Price-to-Earnings Ratio. Stock price divided by annual earnings per share, used to assess if a stock is overvalued or undervalued relative to peers.

## Sector Rotation
Investment strategy of shifting capital between sectors based on economic cycles or market conditions, exploiting relative sector valuations.

## Basis Point
One hundredth of one percent (0.01%), commonly used in interest rate and bond yield discussions for precision.

## Beta
Measure of stock volatility relative to the market index, indicating whether a stock is more volatile (>1) or less volatile (<1) than the market.

## Alpha
The excess return earned on an investment beyond predicted market risk, representing a fund manager's skill in beating the market.

## Equity
Ownership stake in a company through shares, providing voting rights and residual claim on profits.

## Mutual Fund
Pooled investment vehicle managed by professionals, collecting capital from multiple investors and investing in diversified assets.

## Bond
Fixed-income security representing a loan agreement, with predetermined coupon payments and maturity date.

## IPO
Initial Public Offering. First issuance of shares to the public, allowing companies to raise capital and transition from private to public ownership.

## NIM
Net Interest Margin. The difference between interest income and interest expenses as a percentage of earning assets, key profitability metric for banks.

## Credit Rating
Assessment of creditworthiness by agencies like CRISIL, ICRA, indicating default risk and borrowing cost.

## Market Cap
Market Capitalization. Total market value of a company's outstanding shares, calculated as share price multiplied by shares outstanding.

## Liquidity
Ability to quickly convert an investment into cash without significant price concession, important for portfolio flexibility.
